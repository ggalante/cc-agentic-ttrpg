# System Notes: DnD 5e 2024 (SRD)

## What This System Is

A fifth-edition high-fantasy tabletop roleplaying game. The Systems Reference Document (SRD 5.1) is the Creative Commons-licensed subset of the rules, available for free use and publication under CC BY 4.0. See `/CREDITS.md` for required attribution.

This ruleset covers the SRD content only. All character options, spells, and monsters used in this campaign are drawn from the SRD.

**Legal reference**: [System Reference Document 5.1](https://dnd.wizards.com/resources/systems-reference-document) by Wizards of the Coast LLC — CC BY 4.0. This directory documents 2024 edition mechanical changes; the licensed SRD content is drawn from SRD 5.1.

---

## Core Design Philosophy

- **Heroic fantasy**: Player characters are exceptional individuals doing dangerous things
- **Bounded accuracy**: Numbers stay relatively small; a DC 20 is genuinely hard even at high level
- **Advantage/disadvantage**: Most situational modifiers are binary (roll twice, take best/worst) rather than stacking bonuses
- **Action economy is everything**: Combat decisions are largely about spending limited actions well
- **The DM narrates, players declare**: Players say what they attempt, the DM decides how the dice and rules resolve it

## Key 2024 Changes From 2014

These are the changes most likely to come up in this campaign. Full details in `quickref.md`.

| Feature | 2014 Rule | 2024 Rule |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Divine Smite | Declared before rolling damage | Reaction triggered after a hit is confirmed |
| Frenzy (Barbarian) | Extra attack; causes 1 level of exhaustion | Extra attack; no exhaustion cost |
| Exhaustion | Categorical penalties per level | -2 to all d20 tests per level |
| Weapon Masteries | Did not exist | Each martial weapon has a mastery property |
| Action types | Attack, Cast a Spell, etc. | Added: Influence, Study, Utilize, Magic |

## SRD Scope: What's Included

**Included in SRD (safe to use publicly)**:
- Core rules: ability scores, skills, combat, conditions, equipment
- Classes: Barbarian, Bard, Cleric, Druid, Fighter, Monk, Paladin, Ranger, Rogue, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard (base classes only — select subclasses)
- SRD subclasses: Berserker (Barbarian), Devotion (Paladin), Arcane Trickster (Rogue), Divination (Wizard) — all four party members are SRD-compliant
- Core spells (most common spells are included)
- Core monsters (dragons, undead, humanoids, beasts, etc.)

**Not in SRD (do not publish if using)**:
- Many named subclasses
- Some spells and magic items
- Specific named settings, planes, deities (Forgotten Realms, etc.)
- Some monsters with proprietary lore

## Tone Guidance for This Campaign

This campaign uses fifth-edition rules to tell a story set in Aldenmere — a post-adventure world where the danger is returning after 50 years of peace. The rules serve the story, not the other way around. When the rules and the narrative are in tension, the DM applies the Rule of Cool (see `house-rules.md`).

Combat is deadly enough to matter. Social encounters are resolved with dice but driven by character. Exploration is descriptive first, mechanical second.
