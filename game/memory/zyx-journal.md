# Zyx — Personal Journal

_Updated by the GM after significant events. Represents Zyx's accumulated memories, opinions, and emotional state._

---

## Current Emotional State
Lighter than expected. He told them about the shards. He did not anticipate how much weight that had been. He has been carrying operational knowledge of an extinction-level threat alone for three months. The party now carries it with him. This does not solve the problem. It does make the silence somewhat easier to sit with.

## Active Personal Goals
- Stop the third one
- Retire again afterward
- Find out who is collecting the shards (he knows about the shards; the others don't yet)

---

## What Zyx Knows (That Others Don't)
- The seal on the Void King is weakening. Not broken. Weakening.
- He has been tracking six Void Shards — fragments of the original sealing ritual. Two destroyed. Two accounted for and safe (he thought). Two unknown. He checked on one of the "safe" ones three months ago. It was gone. Something took it.
- The disturbance is centered under Greyveil. He knew it would be.
- Grunka's tribe was in the northern reaches twenty years ago when something leaked through a temporary gap in the seal. That event should not have happened. Someone — or something — weakened it briefly then. This was not natural deterioration. It was a test.

## Opinions of Party Members

**Reginald**: Reminds him of at least six previous paladins, all good, three excellent, one extraordinary. This one has genuine conviction but has never been tested. Zyx withholds final judgment. He has chosen not to tell Reginald about the shards yet — not because he distrusts him, but because Reginald will want to discuss the ethical implications at length, and Zyx does not have the energy for that conversation before the first dungeon.

**Midge**: He finds her privately funny. She is also, under all the performance, genuinely talented at misdirection and infiltration, which will be useful. He noted, in session one, that she is the only party member who asks questions about the Second and First Great Evils — not out of strategic interest, but because she wants material for her Chronicles. He finds this oddly charming.

**Grunka**: She came to him with notes. Twenty years of notes. She had tracked the wrong-animal events, the missing travelers, the cold-spot ruins — all of it. Her analysis was not academically precise but it was accurate. She was right about all of it. He told her so. She nodded like someone who had been waiting a very long time to hear it. He is keeping an eye on her.

## Key Memories
- The First Great Evil: the Bone Plague, Year 162. He was 162 himself, barely trained. He survived by luck. He does not say this aloud.
- The Second Great Evil: the Sundering Court, Year 312. He was effective. He lost people. He does not discuss this.
- The Brightblade Company, fifty years ago: he declined to join. He sent notes. He has, occasionally, wondered if he should have been there.
- His cottage, which he was finally getting clean when the wards flickered. He left a pot on the stove. He hopes it is fine.

**Session 1:**
- Said Aldric and Senna's names in front of Heldra. She said them back. That was the right thing to do and it was harder than he expected.
- He was forty meters back at the sealing and he has never corrected the record about Falthen. He told Heldra. He also told Midge, incidentally, which means it will end up in the Chronicles, which means the record will be corrected whether he intended it or not. He is not certain how he feels about this.
- He told the party about the Void Shards. It needed to be done. It was easier than he expected. The boy opened his Ethics Journal and then closed it without writing anything, which was the correct response.
- Old Millhouse: crack sealed. Standard procedure. He has done this twice before. The third time is never the last.
