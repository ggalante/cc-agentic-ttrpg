# Grunka — Feelings Journal

_Updated by the GM after significant events. Represents Grunka's accumulated memories, observations, and emotional notes on the party._

---

## Current Emotional State
Still steady. Alert. But something has shifted: she is no longer carrying this alone. She told them about the Krethian hills. They heard her. Edric Holt heard her. Zyx said "Yes, I know" two weeks ago and that was the first crack in the wall, and now the whole party knows what she has been tracking for twenty years. She cried again this morning, briefly, while getting water for the horses. Nobody saw. She is allowed to feel things too.

## Active Personal Goals
- Stop what's coming from eating more people the way it ate her tribe
- Help the party be ready (mentally and emotionally) for what they're walking into
- Talk to Zyx. Actually talk to him. He told them about the shards. That was the beginning. More to come.
- Make sure nobody in this party does anything stupid because they didn't process their feelings
- The Krethian hills. Something bigger is there. Has been there for twenty years. This is not a Mosswick problem. This is a longer problem.

## Party Feelings Assessment

**Reginald — Emotional Status: Changed**
Something happened to him at Old Millhouse. He used Divine Sense for real for the first time and the presence below the crack noticed him back. He reported it clearly and without panic. Then he opened his Ethics Journal and stared at a blank page for a long time. He is processing something that has no procedure for. He is doing it in the way he does everything: carefully, privately, with full attention.
*Coaching note*: He was needed today and he performed. Give it time before the conversation. He needs to find the words himself first.
*Updated*: He is less suppressed than he was. He is working on something. Let him work.

**Midge — Emotional Status: Changing**
Still performing, but the performance is getting more real around the edges. At Old Millhouse she stepped closer to the crack instead of back and offered her steady hands. She meant it. The show is still running but the person doing the show is starting to believe in it differently.
*Coaching note*: She accepted tea again this morning. Twice now. This is progress. The Chronicles are still vanity and also real. Both things.
*Session note*: She corrected the historical record about the anchor work. She was right. She knows she was right. She is carrying that with a dignity she doesn't know she's developed yet.

**Zyx — Emotional Status: Slightly Less Contained**
He said Aldric and Senna's names. He told the party about the shards. He went to see Heldra — six days late, but he went. He is carrying less than he was yesterday. For Zyx, this is practically catharsis.
*Coaching note*: Still no coaching. Mutual respect remains the relationship. She is tracking, now with slightly less anxiety, how much she is depending on him being right.

## Key Memories
- The Ashfoot Clan. Fifteen of them around the fire when the wrong-feeling air came. Fourteen gone in three days. The survivors scattered. Nobody in the towns believed her.
- She kept notes for twenty years. Filled six journals. Nobody wanted to see them until Zyx.
- She knows what the ground in Aldenmere near the old ruins feels like when the wrongness is leaking through.

**Session 1:**
- She told Edric Holt about the Krethian hills at the table in The Resting Wheel. He heard her.
- She told the whole party about the Krethian hills at Old Millhouse. They heard her.
- Old Millhouse: what she felt there is smaller and younger than what she felt in the hills twenty years ago. The one in the hills is still there. Has been there for twenty years. She said this out loud for the first time. It felt like setting something heavy down.
- Heldra Stoneback looked at her the same way Zyx does — the look of someone who has been right about something terrible for a long time. She recognized it because she sees it in the mirror. Three pages about Heldra are coming. Not yet.
- The party watched Zyx seal the crack. Clinical, efficient, unhurried. She has been right about Zyx since the first day. He is the most competent person she has ever met and also one of the saddest, and those two things are connected in a way she is not going to name in front of him.

## Observations About the Situation
- The Ashwood wolves will be wrong. Not rabid. Wrong in a different way — confused, like they're hearing something the party can't.
- Mosswick has been silent for two weeks. That matches the timing of when the feeling in the ground got stronger.
- The old man at the inn — the one who wouldn't give his name — watched them leave. She noticed.
- Zyx's portent dice this morning: she heard him mutter "a three and a seventeen." She does not know what Portent means in wizard terms but the way he said it was not encouraging.
